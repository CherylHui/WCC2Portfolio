How are you today? (2023)

by Wing Hei Cheryl Hui

An interactive meditation booth.

Introduction

Audience can enter the meditation booth to spend sometime with themselves and meditate.


During the meditation, interactive audiovisual elements will be projected into the meditation booth,


the scent of lavender will also come out of the booth and meditative music will be played during the meditation.

Concept and Background Research

This project is an installation which I took meditation as an inspiration. In the past few years, I was struggling with depression and I learnt to meditate.


I found that meditation helps a lot in controlling my emotion and improving self awareness.


Also, I found that self-love is one of the ways to overcome mental challenges.



I have been living in Hong Kong and now in London, both of the cities are very vibrant, people get used to busy lives and forgot about taking care of themselves.


Therefore, I decided to create an installation which people can take some time to do a very short meditation and at the same time raising the awareness of self love.


For the music creation part, I have studied about the Solfeggio frequencies.


These frequencies are specific tones that claimed to be beneficial towards the mind and body in specific ways.


Technical Implementation


From technical aspect, this project can mainly divided into three parts, serial communication between Arduino and p5.js, audio analysis and audio reactive graphics.


The serial communication between Arduino and p5.js sends heartbeat rate data to the sketch and responsible for the colour change of the graphics. During the design


process, I have tried to use the heartbeat rate data for the rotation of the graphic. However, the effect was not satisfying.


Therefore, I decided to use the heartbeat rate to determine the colour of the graphics.


The audio analysis process utilised the FFT function. The function divided the audio file into 5 different spectrum, the bass, lowMid, Mid, highMid and tremble.


I utilised the data for the transformation of the graphics.


At the same time, the graphic rotates according to the frame rate to create an illusion of 3D graphics.



Finally, a GUI is added to help with matching the colour for the projection and also mirroring the text.

Reflection and Future Development

During the creation process of the meditation booth, I stretched my limit quite a lot.


In graphics design wise, I learnt to employ polar coordinates in my drawing, utilising



mathematics properties such as sin and cos to create some symmetric and smooth edges graphics.



Nevertheless, I learnt to create glowing graphics by adding shadow to the drawing. This allows me to create some drawing with mellower feeling than rigid lines and curves only.


Apart from the design, the construction of the booth is also challenging, I have refined my design and gave up on using projector in the end.


Here is the initial design for the meditation booth, I planned to project the images from the side of the meditation booth and onto a holographic film.


However, the mapping becomes really difficult as the sides of the box may block out some of the projections.

Therefore, I changed the projection into using the Pepper ghost method using the iPad as the light source and a clear plastic film for the images to form on.


These are the footages of experimenting with the set up and the updated design.

If I have a chance to further develop this project, I would like to expand this meditation and self caring series into different form of installation as well as performances.


Firstly, I would like to apply the audio reactive images in sound bath setting. Which I think having


some visual aids may help people who are not used to calm themselves through meditative methods to try and experience the peaceful moments


Nevertheless, I think we may also further develop this 2D design into a 3D ones and create an immersive soundscape as well via 3JS.


This may allows me to further develop the 3JS environment during the mid-term project.


Lastly, I would like to build some more physical set up that is controlled by the heartbeat of the audience.


For example, a beater may hit some singing bowls or gongs according to the biometric data collected from the audience, to provide a personal meditative environment.
